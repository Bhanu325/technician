Creating a website for a washing machine (and refrigerator) repair technician can be simple and cost-effective. Here’s a step-by-step guide to help you set up a professional website — even if you’re not a web developer.

✅ Step 1: Choose a Website Builder or Platform

For ease and affordability, use a website builder like:

  • Wix – Easy drag-and-drop, good for beginners.

  • Squarespace – Sleek design, great templates.

  • WordPress (with Elementor or other page builders) – More flexible, ideal if you want long-term control.

  • Google Business Profile website – Free, simple one-page site linked to your business profile.

✅ Step 2: Pick a Domain Name

Choose something short, relevant, and easy to remember. Examples:

  • QuickWashRepair.com

  • YourCityApplianceFix.com

  • JohnsFridgeFix.com

Buy it from providers like:

  • GoDaddy

  • Namecheap

  • Google Domains

✅ Step 3: Design the Website

Focus on these key pages:

1. Home Page

  • Brief intro to your service.

  • Clear call-to-action (e.g., "Call Now", "Request a Quote").

  • Service area (e.g., "Serving [City/Region]").

2. About Page

  • Short bio or company story.

  • Years of experience.

  • What makes your service trustworthy and different.

3. Services Page

List services like:

  • Washing machine repair (front-load, top-load)

  • Refrigerator repair (single-door, double-door, inverter models)

  • Emergency or same-day service

Include brand names you specialize in (LG, Samsung, Whirlpool, etc.).

4. Contact Page

Include:

  • Phone number (click-to-call)

  • WhatsApp or text link

  • Email address

  • Contact form

  • Google Maps location (if you have a workshop)

5. Testimonials (Optional but Helpful)

Show customer reviews if you have them — builds trust quickly.

✅ Step 4: Make It Mobile-Friendly

Ensure your website looks great on phones — most users will be mobile.

✅ Step 5: Set Up Google Tools

  • Google Business Profile – Appear in local searches.

  • Google Maps Pin – So customers can find you.

  • Google Analytics – Track visits to your website.

✅ Step 6: Add a Booking or Quote Request Option

If you want to take service requests online, use tools like:

  • Calendly (for appointments)

  • Tidio or WhatsApp chat plugins (for live chat)

  • Contact Form with fields like name, address, appliance issue

✅ Step 7: Promote Locally

  • Add your website to your Google Business profile.

  • Share on local Facebook groups, WhatsApp, and neighborhood directories.
